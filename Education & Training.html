Potential Postsecondary Programs

In order to become a software developer, you will most likely need to take a computer science or a computer
engineering program. Below are a few postsecondary programs and the requirements to get into them.

School Program Cost Length
University of Waterloo Computer Engineering (Co-op) $17 000 / year 5 Years
University of Toronto Computer Science $6 100 / year 4 Years
University of Queen's Computer Engineering $14 515 / year 4 Years
University of Guelph Computer Science (Co-op) $5 544.38 / year 4 years

All of these programs require the following:

  • A minimum average of low 80s
  • Ontario Secondary School Diploma (OSSD)
  • Grade 12 English (university)
  • Grade 12 Calculus and Vectors
  • Grade 12 Chemistry (university)
  • Grade 12 Physics (university)
  • Grade 12 Advanced Functions (university)
  • One additional grade 12 university level course

Experiential Learning

Co-op programs are a great way to get experience. For example, you could take the computer engineering co-op program at University of Waterloo or the computer science co-op program at University of Guelph. By taking a co-op program, you can graduate while getting 1-2 years of work experience and pay.
